1874 Arnold Schönberg born on September 13 in Vienna. Eldest child of Jewish parents, Samuel and Pauline Schönberg.

1876 Birth of sister, Ottilie.

1880 Enters primary school.

1882 Birth of brother, Heinrich. Begins to learn violin.

1883 First attempts at compostion.

1885 Enters secondary school. Meets with, befriends and learns music theory from Oskar Adler. Teaches himself cello and begins playing chamber music with friends.

1889 Death of father, Samuel Schönberg.

1890 Leaves school without completing diploma to help support family. Begins work as bank clerk.

1891 or 1892 Befriends David Josef Bach.

1893 Plays cello in amateur orchestra under first composition teacher, Alexander von Zemlinsky. Wins first composition prize for song 'Schilflied'.

1894 Composition of Three Piano Pieces.

1895 Gives up job at bank. Decides to devote himself entirely to music. Begins to conduct choruses of factory workers.

1896 Composition of Six Pieces for Piano Four Hands, Serenade for Small Orchestra and a number of songs.

1897 Earns money orchestrating and arranging other composer's works. Composition and premiere in Vienna of String Quartet in D major. Composition of

1898 Converts to Protestantism. Composition of Two Songs op. 1.

1899 Conducts men's chorus in Heiligenstadt. Composition of Four Songs op. 2 and the string sextet, Verklärte Nacht (Transfigured Night).

1900 Meets Alma Schindler, future wife of Gustav Mahler.

1901 Marries Mathilde von Zemlinsky, sister of Alexander. .

1902 Birth of daughter Gertrud. Orchestrates operettas to earn more money. Meets Richard Strauss

1903 Meets and begins correspondence with Ferruccio Busoni. Contract with first publisher, Verlag Dreililien, Berlin. Returns to Vienna

1904 Meets Gustav Mahler. Founds, with Zemlinsky, Vereinigung schaffender Tonkünstler (Society of Creative Composers) with Mahler as honorary president. Alban Berg, Anton Webern and Erwin Stein become private students. Completes Six Orchestral Songs op. 8. Begins First String Quartet in D minor op. 7.

1905 Adolf Loos secretly finances concerts of Schönberg's works. Completes First String quartet and Eight Songs op. 6. Conducts premiere of Pelleas und Melisande in Vienna.

1906 Birth of son Georg. Composition of op. 9. Begins work on op. 38 (set aside and not completed until 1939).

1907 Begins experimenting with painting. Meets and befriends Richard Gerstl.

1908 Wife, Mathilde, leaves Schönberg and her two children for Gerstl. Mathilde returns to Schönberg. Gerstl commits suicide. First concert of music by Schönberg's students.

1909 Publishes first of many articles: About Music Criticism. Contract with Universal Edition, Vienna.

1910 First exhibition of Schönberg's paintings at Galerie Heller, Vienna, supplemented by concerts of his music. Lectures and teaches at the Royal Acadamey of Music in Vienna. Begins writing Harmonielehre (Theory of Harmony).

1911 Begins correspondence with Wassily Kandinsky.

1912 Teaches composition in Berlin. Edward Steuermann, Edward Clark become students.

1913 Awarded the first Mahler Stipend.

1914 The First World War begins.

1915 Conducts Beethoven's Ninth Symphony in Vienna. Moves back to Vienna. Volunteers for Austian army.

1916 Discharged from army as medically unfit.

1917 Recalled to army, but discharged permanently on health grounds.

1918 The First Wold War ends.

1919 Hanns Eisler, Rudolf Kolisch and Karl Rankl become students. Egon Schiele paints Schönberg's portrait.
